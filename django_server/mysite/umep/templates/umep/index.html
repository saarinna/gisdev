How to interpret the results?

The map represents outdoor thermal comfort, more specifically, the mean radiant temperature (MRT). MRT refers to the average temperature of all the surfaces surrounding a person. It is influenced by solar radiation, air temperature and temperatures of nearby objects. Thus, for instance, built morphology and green and blue infrastructure affect the values.

The ideal and comfortable outdoor MRT values depend on climate, environmental factors (e.g. wind and humidity), shading and personal preferences. Generally speaking, the ideal outdoor MRT for thermal comfort often ranges between 18°C to 29°C based on studies.

These results could be used for planning and improving green or blue infrastructure, and finding urban areas where improvements are needed. For example, increasing tree cover and green space promotes evaporative cooling and provides shade.
